Schokoladen Konsum durch die Welt

by Svetlin Ivanov

Too bad: In 2020 there will be no more chocolate! That’s what candy apocalyptists claim. Their concern is rooted in the increasing demand for cocoa beans in developing and emerging countries. At the same time, the size of arable farmland decreases worldwide because of climate change.


The coveted bean is mainly grown in Africa, where 77 percent of all cocoa beans worldwide are harvested. The Ivory Coast is the world market leader in cocoa production providing 40 percent of all beans. Without cocoa beans there would be no more chocolate as cocoa is its main ingredient.


The end of chocolate would be especially hard to deal with for Europeans. They consume almost half of all chocolate products worldwide (48 %). North and South Americans combined eat or drink only 33 percent of all chocolate products.
