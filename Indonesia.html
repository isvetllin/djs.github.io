Warum soll Mark Zuckerberg unbedingt noch mal nach Indonesien fliegen?

by Svetlin Ivanov

Apart from speaking Mandarin at Tsinghua University, taking a few photos at Borobodur temple and posing with the newly-elected Indonesian president Joko Widodo in an “uncharacteristic suit” in front of a Batik shop, Zuckerberg has a very special list of reasons for being interested in Asia and Indonesia in particular as a potential market for expansion. The reasons, that stand behind his visit one month ago are not only business-related, since they merge with the aim that Zuckerberg’s non-profit, non-governmental organization Internet.org has set. What is more important, many of those goals are not short-term ones, but instead require long way to go and many reforms and policies to be implemented and backed by local authorities.

Here is a list of the reasons, why Mark Zuckerberg might have to visit Indonesia again very soon

1. Top Growing Tech Market

Indonesia is the only country, listing a company in Bloomberg’s top 10 lists of world’s fastest growing tech companies. In the beginning of the 2nd quarter of 2014 Inovisi Infracom was at the 5th place of world’s fastest growing tech giants and it was the only non-Chinese company in this list. Along with other factors, this automatically puts Indonesia in the list of the fastest developing IT markets in the world.

2. Battlefield for Social Media Giants

East Asia has long ago been a battlefield for social media giants like Facebook and Twitter. Whoever wants to make a step to dominate world market starts from here. Mark Zuckerberg has recently made a statement, that he is very proud of former employees leaving his company to found a new one. A clear reference to people as his former colleague and Facebook co-founder Dave Morin who founded PATH. However, Facebook may lag behind Path, as Typical Path user spends 20 minutes, whereas typical Facebook user spends only 17 online, according to Forbes . Therefore whoever wants to dominate the market, must win on this battlefield and the Indonesian capital is one of the most important, since…

3. Jakarta: The city that tweets the most

Jakarta is the most tweeting city, but not the city with most Facebook users. Although, Jakarta might be the most twitter-savvy place on earth Indonesia still faces problems with the Internet penetration. But here comes Internet.org.

4. Internet.org and the Penetration:

Internet.org is Zuckerberg’s NGO aimed at promoting the benefits of better internet penetration in countries like Indonesia. In many regions in the world’s biggest Musim country, the Internet penetration is less than 20% Which means it is way below the share of people connected to the internet in a global scale; 1/3 of world’s population. This interactive heat map shows, that there is a huge gap between the different regions in the country.

5. INTERNET Equals economic Growth:

Samuel Pangerapan, chairman at Indonesia’s Chairman of the Association of Indonesian Internet Providers (APJI), said that if government and private sector put efforts together, the Internet services’s contribution to Indonesian GDP might lead to reaching $26 billion GDP by 2016. The estimation was based on the assumption that the number of Indonesian Internet users will reach 120 million in 2016 from 71.2 million Internet users at the end of 2013.

According to official from Indonesia’s Development Planning Ageny (Bappenas)
10% more internet users = 1.2% GDP growth

All this might mean, that Indonesia’s future is impossible without proper policy of investment in the IT sector.
